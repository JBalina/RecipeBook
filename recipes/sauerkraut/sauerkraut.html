Sauerkraut

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cabbage
  • Salt, as needed
  • Optional: 1 tbsp Caraway seeds
  • Optional: 1 tsp Juniper berries

Instructions:

  1. Quarter and core the cabbage. Shred into very thin strips.
  2. Weigh the cabbage. The salt needed is exactly 2% of the weight of the cabbage.
  3. In a large bowl, add in the cabbage and salt. Aggressively massage and squeeze the salt into the cabbage. The cabbage should release a lot of liquid.
  4. Place the cabbage and its liquid into a half-gallon jar along with the caraway seeds or juniper berries. Push down heavily on the cabbage until the cabbage is completely submerged in its brine. Place plastic wrap and a weight into the jar to weigh down the cabbage, ensuring that nothing floats to the surface.
  5. Loosely seal the jar with a lid and place in a dark, room temperature location. Let ferment for about 3 weeks, checking occasionally that everything is completely submerged in the brine. Once it has the desired taste, transfer to a refrigerator. Serve raw or cooked.
