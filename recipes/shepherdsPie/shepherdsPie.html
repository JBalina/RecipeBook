Shepherd’s Pie

Ingredients:

Meat Filling:

  • 1 lb Ground lamb
  • 1 Onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 2 tsp Dried parsley
  • 1 tsp Dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp Dried thyme
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp Tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp Flour
  • 1 cup Beef broth
  • 1 1/2 Frozen mixed vegetables: Corn, Carrots, Peas

Mashed Potatoes Topping:

  • 1 1/2 - 2 Russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 8 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup Half and half
  • 1/2 tsp Garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/4 tsp Black pepper
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Then add in the onions. Sauté until soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add in the ground meat. Break down the meat with a wooden spoon and add in the dried herbs, salt, and pepper. Mix and cook until completely browned, about 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the Worcestershire sauce and garlic. Stir to combine and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the flour and tomato paste. Stir thoroughly until no clumps of tomato paste remain.
  5. Add the broth and frozen vegetables. Then bring the liquid to a boil and then reduce to simmer. Simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Then remove from heat.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  7. Add the potatoes to a pot with water to cover. Heat to a boil and then reduce to a simmer. Cook until fork tender, 15-25 minutes.
  8. Strain the water out and then return the potatoes to the pot. Let rest for 1 minutes for any remaining water to evaporate. Add in the butter, half and half, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mash until a smooth consistency.
  9. Add in the parmesan and mix until thoroughly incorporated.
  10. place the meat mixture into a 9x9 inch baking dish in an even layer. Spoon the mashed potatoes on top of the meat and carefully spread into an even layer.
  11. Place into the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the meat is bubbling and the potatoes have browned. Then remove from oven and let cool for 15 minutes before serving.
