Red Chili Pork

Ingredients:

Pork:

  • 3 lb Pork butt or Shoulder
  • 2 Onions, diced
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp Cumin
  • 2 tsp Chili powder
  • 2 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Dried oregano
  • 1 tsp Black pepper
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • Water, as needed, at least 4 cups

Red Chili Sauce:

  • 8 Dried california chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 4 cups Pork broth, from cooking the pork
  • 2 tsp Dried oregano
  • 2 tsp Cumin
  • 2 tsp Chili powder
  • 2 tsp Onion powder
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 2 tbsp Canola oil
  • 2 tbsp Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Salt

Instructions:

  1. Add all of the pork ingredients into a slow cooker with enough water to cover the pork. Let cook on low for 8 hours.
  2. Turn off the slow cooker. Remove the pork and strain out the liquid, reserving 4 cups of broth. Shred the pork with forks and return to the slow cooker pot with the onions and garlic. Remove the bay leaves.
  3. Place the dried chilies and broth into a large saucepan. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer. Cook uncovered for 2 minutes.
  4. Transfer the chilies and broth to a blender and add in the oregano, cumin, chili powder, onion powder, and garlic. Blend until very smooth.
  5. Heat the oil in the sauce pan over medium heat. Add in the flour and salt and stir constantly. Strain the blended chili sauce through a sieve and into the saucepan. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes. Taste and add salt to taste.
  6. Transfer the cooked chili sauce to the slower cooker pot with the pork and stir to combine. Serve hot in tacos or in tamales or on its own over rice.
