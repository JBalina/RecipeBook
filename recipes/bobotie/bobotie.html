Bobotie

Ingredients:

Bobotie:

  • 1 1/2 lb Lean ground beef
  • 2 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 2 Onions, diced
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp Curry powder
  • 3 Cloves
  • 2-4 tbsp Blatjang, or to taste
  • 1 tbsp White vinegar, or any preferred vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 Beef stock cube
  • 2 slices White bread, soaked in 1/2 cup Milk
  • Recommended: 1/4 cup Raisins or Sultanas
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Egg Custard Topping:

  • 1 Egg
  • 1/2 cup Milk
  • Optional: 2 tbsp Heavy cream
  • 1/8 tsp Salt, or to taste
  • 1/8 tsp Black pepper, freshly grated, or to taste
  • 5-6 Bay leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Let the bread sliced soak in the milk.
  3. In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Saut√© the onions, garlic, and ginger until the onions begin to brown.
  4. Add in the ground beef, cloves, curry powder, blatjang, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, and beef stock cube. Mix and cook until the meat is completely brown. Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed.
  5. When the meat is brown, mix in the bread soaked in milk and the raisins. When mixed thoroughly, remove from heat.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish and bake, covered, in the oven for 40 minutes.
  7. While cooking in the oven, prepare the egg custard topping. Whisk together the egg, milk, and heavy cream if using. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Remove bobotie from the oven and remove cover. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the bobotie. Arrange the bay leaves on top and then return to the oven for a further 15 minutes.
  9. It is done when the surface of the custard is golden brown. Serve with rice and extra blatjang.
