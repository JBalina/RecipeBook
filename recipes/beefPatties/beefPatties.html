Beef Patties

Ingredients:

Dough:

  • 420 g Flour
  • 2 tsp Course salt
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 cup Butter, cubed, frozen
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 tsp White vinegar
  • 3/4 cup Water, ice cold

Beef Patties:

  • 1 lb Ground beef, 20% fat
  • 2 tbsp Canola oil
  • 1 small Onion, diced
  • 6 cloves Garlic, minced
  • Optional: 1 inch Ginger, finely minced
  • 1 Scoth bonnet, stemmed, minced
  • 1 tbsp Allspice
  • 1 1/2 tsp Black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Paprika
  • 2 cups Low sodium beef broth
  • 1/2~ tsp Salt, to tast
  • 4 Scallions, minced
  • 1 tbsp Fresh thyme
  • 1 Egg + 2 tbsp Water

Instructions:

  1. Place the flour, turmeric, and salt into a large food processor and pulse until homogenous. Then add in the butter and pulse again until well mixed.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the egg, vinegar, and ice water. Then add it into the food processor and do small pulses until it all combines together into a smooth ball of dough. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and place into the fridge to chill for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the beef and break it into small pieces with a wooden spoon. Sauté until cooked through, about 5 minutes. Then transfer the beef to a separate plate, reserving the leftover fat in the pan.
  4. Add the onion, garlic, ginger, and scotch bonnet into the pan and sauté over medium heat until softened, about 3 minutes. Then add the allspice, paprika, and black pepper and sauté until fragrant, 30-60 seconds. Add in the cooked beef and the beef broth and reduce the heat to a simmer. Let cook, stirring occasionally, until the broth has reduced by about half, about 5-10 minutes.
  5. Taste the beef and add salt as needed. Then remove from heat and stir in the scallions and thyme.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  7. Lightly flour a flat workspace. Roll out the dough into about a 20x14 inch rectangle. Then divide and cut the dough into about 8 5x7 inch rectangles.
  8. Fill a rectangle of dough with some beef, leaving a 1/2 inch border. Brush the edges of the rectangle with some water. Then fold over the rectangle and use a fork to press down on the edges and seal the patty. Repeat for all the patties.
  9. Place the patties onto a parchment paper lined baking sheet. Whisk together the egg and 2 tablespoons water to make an egg wash. Brush the surface of each of the patties with egg wash. Transfer to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes or until completely cooked through.
  10. Let cool 5 minutes before serving. Serve hot.
