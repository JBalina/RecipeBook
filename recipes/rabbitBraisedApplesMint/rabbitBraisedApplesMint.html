Rabbit Braised with Apples and Mint

Ingredients:

  • 3 lb Rabbit, cut into pieces
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp Sunflower oil
  • 2-3 Wild onions or 1 Onion, diced
  • 3 Sage leaves
  • 4 Mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup Apple cider
  • 1/2 cup Rabbit stock
  • 2 tbsp Maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp Maple vinegar, or substitute Apple cider vinegar with some Maple syrup
  • 2-3 Crabapples or 1 large apple, cored and sliced

Instructions:

  1. Season the rabbit pieces with salt to taste. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sear the rabbit pieces in batches, about 5 minutes per side. Remove from the pan and reserve.
  2. Add the onions, sage, and mint and cook until the onions become translucent, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add the cider and stock to the pan and scrape up any dark bits sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  4. Mix in the maple syrup and vinegar. Then add back in the rabbit. Cover and bring the heat to medium low. Braise for 45-60 minutes, or until tender, turning occasionally.
  5. Uncover and add in the apples. Continue to cook until the sauce has thickened to a desired consistency. Taste and adjust seasonings.
  6. Serve with wild rice or cornmeal mush.
