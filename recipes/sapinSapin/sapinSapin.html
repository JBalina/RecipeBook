Sapin-Sapin

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups (520 g) Glutinous rice flour
  • 2 14 oz cans Coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cup (300 g) Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Macapuno, optionally chopped
  • 1/4 cup Jackfruit, minced
  • 3-6 drops Langka extract, or to preference
  • Optional: 3-6 drops Yellow food coloring
  • 1/2 cup Ube halaya
  • 1/4 tsp Ube extract, or to preference
  • 1 batch Latik

Instructions:

  1. Prepare a very large steamer. Grease a 10" cake pan with coconut oil, preferably the coconut oil from making the latik.
  2. Combine the glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar into a large mixing bowl. Whisk together to combine until homogenous and there are no lumps.
  3. Divide the batter equally into 3 bowls. To one bowl, stir in the macapuno. To the second, stir in the jackfruit, langka extract, and yellow food coloring. To the third, stir in the ube halaya and ube extract.
  4. If using a spring-form pan and not flipping the sapin-sapin to remove it from the pan, start with the white macapuno layer. Otherwise, start with the ube layer. Pour the macapuno/ube batter into the greased cake pan. Transfer to the steamer and let steam for about 8 minutes. Then remove from the steamer and add the langka layer. Return to the steamer for another 8 minutes. Then remove from the steamer and add the final layer. Return to the steamer and steam for about 12-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the middle comes out clean.
  5. Remove it from the steamer. If using a spring-form pan, simply release it from the pan. Otherwise, run a thin, oiled knife along the sides of the pan. Flip it onto a serving plate and tap the pan to help it release.
  6. Top the sapin-sapin with latik. Serve immediately.
