Three Sisters Soup

Ingredients:

  • 3 Wild onions or substitute 1 Onion, diced
  • 1-2 cloves Garlic
  • 3 tbsp Sunflower oil
  • 2 tsp Dried Sage
  • 1 tsp Salt, or to taste
  • 1/8 tsp Cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 2 Acorn squash, 1 halved, 1 cubed and peeled
  • 4-5 ears Corn, kernals only (Use bare cobs for corn stock)
  • 2 cups Hominy, cooked, or 1 can Hominy drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups Beans, cooked, Tepary, Pinto, or any other, or 1 can Beans drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups Corn stock
  • 2 cups Wild rice stock
  • 1 sprig Cedar, or 1 tbsp Dried cedar in a tea ball

Instructions:

  1. Preheat an oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Place the halved acorn squash into the oven and let bake for 1 hour. Remove from oven and let cool.
  2. Heat the oil over medium heat in a large pot. Add the onions and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Mix in the salt, sage, and cayenne. Sauté for 1 minute.
  4. Add in the cubed squash, beans, corn, and hominy. Sauté for 1-3 minutes.
  5. Add in the corn stock, wild rice stock, and cedar. Stir thoroughly. Heat over high heat until it reaches a boil, then lower to a simmer. Let cook for 1 hour.
  6. Place the baked squash into a food processor and blend into a paste. When the soup is done cooking, Remove the cedar from the pot and add in the pureed squash and stir until fully incorporated.
  7. Taste and adjust for seasoning. Serve hot.
