Khachapuri

Ingredients:

Dough:

  • 3 1/2 - 4 cups (420-500 g) Flour, or as needed
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/2 cup Milk,
  • 1 tsp Dry active yeast
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil

Filling Option 1 (More Traditional):

  • 3 1/2 cups Imeruli cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Sulguni Cheese, shredded

Filling Option 2:

  • 1 1/2 cup Farmer's cheese, shredded
  • 1 1/2 cup Feta cheese, shredded
  • 1 1/2 cup Mozzarella, shredded

To Top:

  • 1 Egg + 1 tbsp Water, whisked together for egg wash
  • 4 Eggs

Instructions:

  1. Stir together water, milk, and yeast until well combined. Let rest for 10-15 mintues or until foamy.
  2. Add the flour, salt, and water/milk mixture to a stand mixer and knead until smooth and elastic, about 15 minutes, adding flour if needed. Then add in the olive oil and knead for another minute.
  3. Grease a sealable container with olive oil and place in the dough. Cover and let rise until doubled, about 1 hour.
  4. Preheat an oven to 450 degrees Fahrenheit. Transfer the dough to a heavily floured work surface. Split the dough into 4 equal pieces.
  5. Take one piece of dough and flatten it into a 9 inch circle, dusting with flour as needed. Transfer to a parchment paper-lined baking sheet and top with the cheese filling. Roll in two opposite sides of the circle about 2 inches, optionally stuffing with cheese, and then pinch the corners together. Give the corner ends a gentle twist. Repeat this for all the pieces of dough and cover loosely with plastic wrap. Let proof for 30 minutes.
  6. Once proofed, brush the crusts with the egg wash. Transfer to the oven to bake for 15 minutes. Then briefly remove from the oven and make a small well in the centers of the khachapuris with a spoon. Crack an egg into the center of each khachapuri and return them to the oven. Bake for about 5-6 more minutes, or until the egg is cooked to your liking.
  7. Remove the khachapuri from the oven and serve immediately. Serve hot.
